# Koalas 
![Koalas](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Janani-Ima/MyFirstGitRepo/refs/heads/main/picture/koala.avif)

The Koala, often mistakenly called a “koala bear,” is actually a marsupial native to Australia. It lives mainly in eucalyptus forests, where it spends most of its life in trees. Koalas feed almost entirely on eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrients, so they conserve energy by sleeping for about 18 to 20 hours a day. They have strong limbs and sharp claws that help them climb and hold onto tree branches securely. Female koalas carry their babies, called joeys, in a pouch for several months until they are strong enough to climb on their own. Koalas are generally quiet and solitary animals, but they communicate using low grunting sounds. Today, koalas are considered vulnerable due to threats such as habitat destruction, climate change, and bushfires, which have reduced their natural environment and population in the wild.
